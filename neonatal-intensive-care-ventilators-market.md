<p><ul><li>Invasive</li><li>Non-Invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The neonatal intensive care ventilator market comprises two main types: invasive and non-invasive ventilation. Invasive ventilators are employed for critically ill infants requiring direct airway access, typically via intubation, ensuring precise control over respiratory parameters. In contrast, non-invasive ventilators provide respiratory support without airway insertion, often utilizing methods like continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) or bi-level positive airway pressure (BiPAP). Both types are crucial for managing neonatal respiratory distress and improving outcomes in vulnerable populations.</p></p>
