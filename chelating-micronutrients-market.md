<p><p>The chelating micronutrients market consists of various types, including EDTA (Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid), which effectively binds metal ions to enhance nutrient availability. EDDHA (Ethylenediamine-N,N'-diacetic acid) is especially useful for iron chelation in alkaline soils. DTPA (Diethylenetriaminepentaacetic acid) offers improved stability in diverse pH conditions. IDHA (Iminodisuccinic acid) is environmentally friendly and biodegradable. Other categories may include alternative chelating agents, addressing diverse agricultural needs and improving soil health, plant growth, and nutrient absorption efficiency.</p></p>
