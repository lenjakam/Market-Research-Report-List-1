<p><p>The sail and rowing dinghies market features various types, including coxless pairs and coxed pairs. Coxless pairs consist of two rowers working in sync without a coxswain, often favored for their simplicity and competitive nature. In contrast, coxed pairs include a third crew member, the coxswain, who steers and provides strategic guidance. Both types cater to different preferences and skill levels, appealing to recreational users and competitive rowers, enhancing the overall diversity of the dinghy market.</p></p>
