<p><ul><li>High Vacuum (HV)</li><li>Ultra-high Vacuum (UHV)</li><li>Extreme High Vacuum (XHV)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Vacuum cables for the semiconductor market are categorized based on the level of vacuum they maintain. High Vacuum (HV) cables operate in environments with pressures from 10^-3 to 10^-7 Torr, suitable for many standard applications. Ultra-high Vacuum (UHV) cables achieve pressures below 10^-7 Torr, ideal for advanced processes requiring minimal contamination. Extreme High Vacuum (XHV) cables operate below 10^-9 Torr, essential for cutting-edge research and technologies where even trace gases can affect results. Each type supports specific semiconductor fabrication needs.</p></p>
