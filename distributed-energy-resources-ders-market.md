<p><p>Distributed Energy Resources (DERs) refer to small-scale energy generation and storage technologies located close to where the energy is consumed. In the context of wind DERs, small wind turbines harness kinetic energy from wind to produce electricity, often used for local consumption or feeding into the grid. PV DERs, or photovoltaic distributed energy resources, utilize solar panels to convert sunlight into electricity, providing clean energy solutions. Both markets focus on enhancing energy resilience and reducing reliance on centralized power generation.</p></p>
